Black Pepper Bun

Prep time Difficulty Servings
Min 3.5-4 hours, 45 min active prep + waiting Medium 3 (10 buns)

Dough

  • 450 g bread flour
  • 25 g sugar
  • 1 pack of yeast (7g dry)
  • 25 g lard
  • 225 g cold water
  • NO SALT
  • Optional: sesame oil to coat the dough

Filling

  • 500 g ground pork (padlocks, 10%)
  • 45 g soy sauce
  • 28 g sugar
  • 1,5 g salt
  • 25 g lard
  • 2.5 ts black pepper
  • 2-3 ts oyster sauce
  • 10 g rice vinegar
  • 1 ts of white pepper
  • 1 teaspoon 5spice
  • 1 pinch of chilli cayenne
  • 1 ts chilli flakes
  • 2 grated cloves
  • Ground/ grated ginger
  • 4-5 spring onions

Topping

  • 1-2 egg yolks
  • Sesame seeds
  • Coarse salt
  • Optionally 1:1 honey-water mix instead of the yolks

  1. a, Mix the dough ingredients and let it rest for 45 minutes. Transfer the dough to the fridge, let it rest for 2 hours in a plastic wrap, leaving space for it to rise.
    b, Mix the dough ingredients, put it into a container or plastic bag and oil the dough, transfer it to the fridge, let it rest overnight.
  2. Mix the filling ingredients expcept for the onions, which should be cut into 1 cm pieces.
  3. Divide the dough to 10 pieces (1 bun = approx 68-70 g dough), Stretch it, and fill each with about the same amount of filling (55-60 g) and onions.
    4. Check this video to see how to form the buns.
  4. Preheat you oven to 250 °C.
  5. Coat the buns in egg yolk, or honey-water mix and then dump them in sesame seeds, finally sprinkle the coarse salt on top.
  6. Bake the buns for 15-20 minutes depending on how crispy you want them to be (if your oven doesn't bake evenly, rotate the tray halfway through).

Suggestions:
One person usually eats about 3 buns for lunch or dinner.
Eat while it is hot 🥴
An ordinary nonstick tray can hold 5 buns (leave room for it to expand a bit.)
